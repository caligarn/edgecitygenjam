FACTS & HISTORY

History rhymes.

Every big tech shift looks terrifying until the new creators show up.

  1. 1908

    Hollywood was born from rebellion.

    Filmmakers fled Thomas Edison's patent trust — the MPPC — and moved west to escape enforcement. That migration built the industry that defined the century.

  2. 1984

    The VCR was supposed to kill Hollywood.

    Sony v. Universal let home video live. Within a decade, video rentals earned studios more than the box office.

  3. 2007

    Netflix rewrote TV.

    Streaming turned the living-room set into a global on-demand catalog. Data picked hits. The world binged.

  4. 1999

    Napster couldn't kill music.

    It made streaming inevitable. Today artists earn more from streaming than CDs ever generated.

  5. 2010

    Instagram & Snapchat rewrote photography.

    Phones plus filters plus stories turned billions into daily photographers. Visual culture has never looked the same.

  6. 1975+

    Punk & hip-hop made cheap tools into genres.

    Four-tracks, turntables, photocopiers. Punk built a DIY label system; hip-hop sampled the whole record store. Remixing what already existed turned into two of the most influential movements in music.

WORKING WITH THE TOOLS

AI tips.

Get more from the tools with these habits.

  1. PROMPTS

    Be specific.

    Describe lighting, style, mood, camera angle, and subject in detail. With images, focus on subject, object, landscape, and camera angle. For video, specify one action and one camera movement.

  2. VOLUME

    Iterate fast.

    Generate many options quickly, then pick and refine the best one. Quantity unlocks quality.

  3. REFERENCE

    Use images.

    Upload a style or composition reference to anchor your results. Visual prompts beat words.

  4. MODELS

    Try different ones.

    Each model has its strengths. Switch if one isn’t working — the right tool for the shot.

  5. MOTION

    Keep it subtle.

    Short, simple camera moves work better than complex ones in image-to-video. Less is more.

  6. VIBES

    Embrace artifacts.

    AI weirdness can be a creative feature, not a bug. Lean into the strange — it’s part of the medium.

WORKING WITH THE TEAM

Human tips.

The tools are only as good as the team using them.

  1. ROLES

    Designate clearly.

    Assign Writer, Producer, Editor, and Animator before you start. Everyone knows their lane.

  2. STORY

    Agree first.

    Lock the concept and script before anyone touches the tools. Story leads, tools follow.

  3. PARALLEL

    Work side by side.

    While one person prompts images, another builds the edit timeline. Move on every front at once.

  4. SYNC

    Check in every 20.

    Short syncs every twenty minutes prevent the team from drifting in different directions.

  5. SHIP

    Done > perfect.

    A finished rough cut beats an unfinished masterpiece. Get to the end of the timeline first.

  6. TRUST

    Default to your team.

    If a teammate is closer to the work, trust their call. Speed beats consensus on a two-hour clock.

MY THEORIES
